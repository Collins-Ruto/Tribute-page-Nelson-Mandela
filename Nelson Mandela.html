Nelson Madiba Mandela

Former president of South Africa

Life of Mandela

Early Life

Nelson Mandela was born on 18 July 1918 in th village of Mvenzo in Umtata, South Africa. He was born into the Thembu royal family. His father was a local chief and councillor to the monarch, a position which Mandela later came to be appointed to. He attended a methodist misson school close to the palace.

Political life

Mandela joined University of Witwaterstrand to study law. He faced racism for being the only black student. In 1943, he marched in support of a succesful bus boycott to reverse bus prices. He joined ANC with views that black Africans should be entirely independent in their political struggles.
He nad his party cadre allies began advocating direct action against apartheid. These actions included strikes and boycotts.

Arrest & Imprisonment Robben Island

In June 1952, Mandela addressed a crowd of 10,000, this triggered campaign protests for which he was arrested. These events led to growth of ANC membership from 20,000 to 100,000, whch led to mass arrests.
In August 1962, Mandela was captured along with fellow activists. He was charged with inciting strikes and leaving the country without permission. He was imprisoned, Later in 1964 he was found guilty of four counts of sabotage and conspiracy to overthrow the government and was condemened to life imprisonment in the Robben Island prison. He was kept here for 18 years, during this time he continued studying for his law degree.

Pollsmoor & Victor Verster prisons

In April 1982, he was transfered to Pollsmoor prison in Tokar,Cape Town along with other leaders. His 70th birthday attracted international attention where he was recognised globally as a heroic figure.
In December 1988, he was moved to Victor Verster Prison, near paarl. It was here that he completedd his LLB degree.

Mandela's Freedom & Presidency

F.W. de Klerk took over as president in July 1989. He on 11th February 1990, he released Mandela from prison in a global event. They agreed to negotiate avoiding armed struggle and focus on peace.
In the April 1994 elections, Africans were allowed to participate in the voting process. ANC emerged victorious with 63% of the votes which made Mandela first black president of South Africa. later in the year he published his book Long Walk to Freedom.

Retirement & Death

After fruitful years of presidency where he improved peace, education, health and living conditions of South Africans, Mandela announced his retirement In March 1999. He still remained highly popular according to south africans due to his performance. He continued to spearhead activitisms thereafter.
After suffering from a prolonged respiratory infection, Mandela died on 5th December 2013 at the age of 95.
